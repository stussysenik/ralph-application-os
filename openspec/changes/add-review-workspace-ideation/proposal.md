## Why

Ralph already had benchmark and draft support for screenshot-style products, but the ideation classifier still treated prompts like screenshot studios and review workspaces as generic products unless the operator used very specific vocabulary. That made the front door less honest than the rest of the product.

## What Changes

- add a first-class `review-workspace` software category for screenshot, annotation, and review-oriented products
- classify screenshot studio prompts onto the interactive-runtime path instead of generic fallback
- add review-specific interview pressure around capture lifecycle, annotation behavior, and sharing boundaries
- surface review-workspace improvement suggestions during ideation

## Impact

- screenshot, design-review, and capture-sharing prompts get a stronger semantic shape at intake
- Ralph's ideation surface becomes more truthful for a real family of supported app ideas
- correction-memory playback for screenshot tools now compounds on top of a better primary category
